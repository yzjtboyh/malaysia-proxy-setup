## Common Pitfalls When Working with Malaysia Proxies

A short list of things that derail people:

- **Treating Singapore IPs as "close enough."** They're not. SG and MY are different markets, different pricing, different content versions. If you're researching the Malaysian market, you need MY exits.
- **Ignoring DNS leaks.** Your proxy can route HTTP traffic through Malaysia while your DNS queries go through your ISP's resolver. Sites that fingerprint via DNS will catch this. Use the proxy's DNS or a Malaysia-resolvable resver.
- **Forgetting time zones.** Malaysia is UTC+8. If your scraper logs timestamps in UTC and you compare against a site that uses MYT, you'll think your data is broken when it's just a clock conversion.
- **Burning through bandwidth on uncompressed pages.** Residential is metered. Modern e-commerce pages can hit 3-5MB each with images. A 10,000-page scrape at 4MB per page is40GB. Plan accordingly, or compress and strip non-essential resources before fetching.
